## ✨ THIS WEEK IN THE SKY

🌙 **Waxing Moon**
The Moon is waxing this week following the New Moon on February 17. The growing crescent lights up the early evening sky — a reminder that dark-sky opportunities are limited for now.

☿ **Mercury in the Evening Sky**
Mercury reached greatest eastern elongation on February 19 and is well-placed for viewing this week! Look for it low in the western sky just after sunset. One of the best opportunities of the year to spot the innermost planet.

🌕 **Total Lunar Eclipse — Coming March 3!**
Just TWO days after this week ends, a spectacular **Total Lunar Eclipse (Blood Moon)** graces our skies on the night of March 2–3. Totality is **fully visible from California**:

• Total eclipse begins: **3:04 AM PST**
• Maximum eclipse: **3:33 AM PST**
• Total eclipse ends: **4:02 AM PST**

The Moon will turn a rusty red-orange color during totality. This is the **last total lunar eclipse until late 2028** — don't miss it! No special equipment needed.

🌝 **Worm Moon**
The Full Moon on March 3 is the traditional Worm Moon, named by early Native American tribes for the earthworms that emerge as the ground softens in late winter.
