## 📅 UPCOMING EVENTS

───────────────────────────────

🔭 **Astro 101: So You Want to be an Amateur Astronomer**
📅 Friday, May 8 | 8:00 PM – 9:30 PM PDT (talk starts 8:15 PM)
📍 Houge Park, 3972 Twilight Dr, San Jose

Presenter **Kal Krishnan** gives a comprehensive intro to amateur astronomy — constellations, sky objects, observing conditions, and telescopes. The first 15 minutes feature a live night sky preview using Stellarium, followed by the main talk. Telescopes will be set up outside afterward for the In-Town Star Party.

🔗 https://www.meetup.com/sj-astronomy/events/312673004/

🌌 **In-Town Star Party (ITSP)**
📅 Friday, May 8 | 9:15 PM – 11:15 PM PDT
📍 Houge Park, 3972 Twilight Dr, San Jose

SJAA volunteers share views through their telescopes right in the city. Binoculars welcome; please leave personal telescopes at home. Use a red-filtered flashlight to preserve night vision.

• Want to volunteer? Email itsp@sjaa.net

🔗 https://www.meetup.com/sj-astronomy/events/312795171/

───────────────────────────────

🪐 **Starry Nights Star Party**
📅 Saturday, May 9 | 9:15 PM – 11:15 PM PDT
📍 Rancho Cañada del Oro Open Space Preserve, 4289 Casa Loma Rd, Morgan Hill

Co-hosted by SJAA and the Santa Clara County Open Space Authority. About 30 min south of San Jose with significantly darker skies — dark enough to see the Milky Way. The Moon rises around midnight on May 9, so the full event window is moon-free. **⚠️ Registration required — limited to ~95 cars.**

• Arrive no later than 10:15 PM (1 hr before end)
• Sign in at the OSA table on arrival
• Binoculars welcome; no personal telescopes or tripods
• Dress in warm layers — it gets cold!
• No pets, no smoking, no flash photography
• Check the Meetup page for weather updates before heading out

🔗 https://www.meetup.com/sj-astronomy/events/311917988/
