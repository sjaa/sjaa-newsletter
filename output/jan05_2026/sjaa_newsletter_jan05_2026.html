ü™ê Jupiter at Opposition - January 10

This week's highlight! Jupiter reaches opposition, meaning it's at its closest approach to Earth and will be visible all night long from sunset to sunrise. This is the best time of the year to observe the giant planet and its four Galilean moons through telescopes and binoculars.

üå† Quadrantid Meteor Shower

While the peak occurred on January 3-4, the first major meteor shower of 2026 continues to be active during the early part of this week. Early risers may still catch some shooting stars.

üåí Darkening Skies

With the New Moon approaching on January 18, skies are getting progressively darker throughout this week, providing excellent conditions for observing faint deep-sky objects and the Milky Way.

‚òÄÔ∏è Solar Sunday

Date: Sunday, January 11, 2026

Time: 1:30 PM - 3:30 PM

Location: Houge Park, 3972 Twilight Dr, San Jose, CA

Given the bad weather on the 4th, this event is a second attempt at the first solar observing session of 2026! Cross your fingers for sunny skies!

Our Sun provides energy for all life on Earth - now it's time to give it a closer look! Join SJAA for amazing and detailed views using special telescopes that keep your eyes perfectly safe.

What You'll See:

  • White-light telescopes with dense solar filters revealing sunspots
  • Hydrogen-alpha telescopes showing prominences (solar flares) and chromosphere texture
  • Learn how the Sun works and how magnetic fields drive solar activity
  • Short informal introduction at 1:30 PM
  • Solar system scale demonstration - take one home in your pocket!

What to Bring:

  • Hat, sunscreen, and water - it may be hot!
  • Your own properly filtered telescope if you want to share views (arrive by 1:00 PM to set up)

16 members attending! Free and open to the public - perfect for all ages.
