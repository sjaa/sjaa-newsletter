## ✨ THIS WEEK IN THE SKY

🌖 **Waning Moon — Dark Skies Ahead**
The April Pink Moon rose on April 2nd and is now waning. Skies darken progressively through the week, building toward the **New Moon on April 17** — prime deep-sky observing is just around the corner!

☿ **Mercury in the Morning Sky**
Mercury reached greatest western elongation (27.8°) on April 3rd — this week is still an excellent window to spot it **low on the eastern horizon just before sunrise**. One of the best Mercury apparitions of the year!

🌠 **Lyrids Meteor Shower — Plan Ahead!**
The Lyrid shower runs April 16–25, peaking the **night of April 22/23** (~20 meteors/hour). The first-quarter moon sets after midnight, leaving dark skies for what should be a great show. Meteors radiate from Lyra but appear anywhere in the sky. Mark your calendars!
