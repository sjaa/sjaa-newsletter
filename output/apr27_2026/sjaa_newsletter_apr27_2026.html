✨ This Week in the Sky

🌕 Full Moon (Flower Moon) — Friday, May 1

The Moon will be fully illuminated on May 1 at 17:24 UTC (10:24 AM PDT). Early Native American tribes called this the Flower Moon, marking the time when spring flowers bloom in abundance. It is also known as the Corn Planting Moon and the Milk Moon. The bright moonlight this week will wash out fainter objects, so focus on the Moon, planets, and bright star clusters.

🌠 Eta Aquarids Meteor Shower — Building Toward Peak

The Eta Aquarids shower (active April 19 – May 28) is building toward its peak on the nights of May 6–7. This is an above-average shower capable of up to 60 meteors per hour at peak, produced by debris from Comet Halley. In our Northern Hemisphere location, expect rates around 30 per hour near peak. Meteors radiate from the constellation Aquarius but can appear anywhere in the sky. Best viewing is from a dark location after midnight.

☀️ Solar Sunday — See Our Sun Up Close (In Person)

Date: Sunday, May 3, 2026

Time: 2:00 PM – 4:00 PM PDT

Location: Houge Park, 3972 Twilight Dr, San Jose, CA

Get amazing, safe views of the Sun through SJAA's specially filtered telescopes! White-light scopes reveal sunspots, while hydrogen-alpha scopes show prominences and intricate chromospheric detail. A short informal talk at approximately 1:35 PM; come anytime to enjoy the views and ask questions. A short walk-through of solar system scale is also on offer.

If you have a properly filtered white-light or H-alpha telescope, you're welcome to bring it and share views with others — please arrive by 1:00 PM to set up. Come prepared with a hat, sunscreen, and water.
