✨ This Week in the Sky

🌕🔴 Total Lunar Eclipse — March 3

This is the astronomical event of the season! A total lunar eclipse occurs on the night of March 3rd as the Moon passes completely through Earth's dark shadow (umbra). Watch as the Moon gradually darkens and takes on a dramatic rusty red or "blood moon" color — a stunning sight with the naked eye or any telescope.

Visibility: Visible from North America, making this a perfect opportunity to observe from your own backyard or join fellow members for a viewing session.

Note: This is the last total lunar eclipse until the very end of 2028 — don't miss it!

🌕 Full Moon (Worm Moon) — March 3

The Full Moon coincides with the total lunar eclipse, reaching peak fullness at 11:39 UTC. This moon is traditionally called the Worm Moon by early Native American tribes, marking the time when the ground begins to soften and earthworms reappear. It's also been called the Crow Moon, Crust Moon, Sap Moon, and Lenten Moon.

☿ Mercury in the Evening Sky

Mercury is currently visible in the evening sky. Look for the planet low on the western horizon just after sunset. Catch it while you can — Mercury is notoriously brief in its appearances!

Date: Thursday, March 5, 2026

Journey through the winter chapter of a four-part seasonal expedition across the cosmos!
