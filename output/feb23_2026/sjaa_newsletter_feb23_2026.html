✨ This Week in the Sky

☾ Waxing Moon
The Moon is waxing this week following the New Moon on February 17. The growing crescent will light up the early evening sky — a beautiful sight but a reminder that dark-sky opportunities are limited until later in the month.

♀ Mercury in the Evening Sky
Mercury reached its greatest eastern elongation on February 19 and is now well-placed for viewing. Look for it low in the western sky just after sunset. This is one of the best opportunities of the year to spot the innermost planet!

🌙 Total Lunar Eclipse — Coming March 3!
Mark your calendars — just TWO days after this week ends, a spectacular Total Lunar Eclipse (Blood Moon) graces our skies on the night of March 2–3. Totality is fully visible from California:

  • Total eclipse begins: 3:04 AM PST
  • Maximum eclipse: 3:33 AM PST
  • Total eclipse ends: 4:02 AM PST

The Moon will turn a rusty red-orange color during totality. This is the last total lunar eclipse until late 2028 — don't miss it! No special equipment needed; your eyes are all it takes.

🌠 Worm Moon
The Full Moon on March 3 is the traditional Worm Moon — named by early Native American tribes for the earthworms that emerge as the ground softens in late winter.

🔭 Solar Sunday — See Our Sun Up Close

Date: Sunday, March 1, 2026

Time: 2:00 PM – 4:00 PM

Location: Houge Park, 3972 Twilight Dr, San Jose, CA

Attendance: 36 members signed up!

Join SJAA for amazing and detailed views of the Sun through special solar-filtered telescopes. We'll have white-light telescopes revealing sunspots, and hydrogen-alpha telescopes showing prominences and intricate chromosphere detail — all completely safe for your eyes!

A short informal introductory talk starts just after 1:30 PM. Bring your own solar-filtered telescope and arrive by 1:00 PM if you'd like to set up and share views.

Tip: Bring a hat, sunscreen, and water — conditions may be warm!
