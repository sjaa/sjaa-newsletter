**January 13 - Moon at Apogee**
The Moon reaches its orbit's furthest point from Earth, appearing fractionally smaller than at other times of the month.

**January 14 - Lunar Occultation of Antares**
The Moon will pass in front of Antares (Alpha Scorpii). This occultation will be visible from Australia.

**January 17 - NGC 2451 Well Placed**
The open star cluster NGC 2451 (magnitude 2.8) in Puppis culminates around midnight local time, making it an excellent target for observation.

**January 18 - New Moon ğŸŒ‘**
Perfect dark skies! The New Moon disappears into the Sun's glare, providing ideal conditions for deep sky observing. No moonlight will hinder your view of stars, galaxies, and nebulae.
