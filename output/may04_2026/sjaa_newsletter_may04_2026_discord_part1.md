## ✨ THIS WEEK IN THE SKY

🌠 **Eta Aquarid Meteor Shower Peak — Wednesday, May 6**
One of the year's best showers peaks this week! Debris from Halley's Comet can produce 40–50 meteors/hour under ideal conditions. Best viewing is 2–3 hours before dawn looking east. The Moon is a waning gibbous, so keep it behind you to maximize your view. No equipment needed — just a reclining chair and dark-adapted eyes.

⭑ **Eta Lyrid Meteor Shower Peak — Friday, May 8**
A minor shower (ZHR ~3) with its radiant in Lyra, peaking the same night as our In-Town Star Party. Watch for the occasional bright sporadic while observing at Houge Park.

🌙 **Last Quarter Moon — Saturday, May 9**
The Moon rises around midnight on May 9, keeping the early evening sky beautifully dark for the Starry Nights Star Party. New Moon follows on May 16 — darker skies ahead!
