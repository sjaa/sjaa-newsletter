✨ This Week in the Sky

🌠 Eta Aquarid Meteor Shower Peak — Wednesday, May 6

One of the year’s best meteor showers peaks this week! The Eta Aquariids originate from debris left by Halley’s Comet and can produce up to 40–50 meteors per hour under ideal conditions. The radiant rises in the east in the early morning hours, so the best viewing is in the 2–3 hours before dawn on May 6. Note that the Moon is a waning gibbous (about 5 days past full) and will be present in the sky overnight. No equipment needed — just a reclining chair and dark-adapted eyes.

🌠 Eta Lyrid Meteor Shower Peak — Friday, May 8

A minor shower peaking on the same night as our In-Town Star Party. The radiant is in Lyra, rising high in the northeast through the evening. Rates are low (ZHR ~3), but you may catch a bright sporadic while observing at Houge Park.

🌙 Last Quarter Moon — Saturday, May 9

The Moon reaches Last Quarter on Saturday, rising around midnight. This means the early evening sky for the Starry Nights Star Party will be nicely dark — ideal for deep-sky objects. We’re also just two weeks from New Moon (May 16), so conditions are improving for dark-sky observing.

🌟 Starry Nights Star Party

Date: Saturday, May 9, 2026

Time: 9:15 PM – 11:15 PM PDT

Location: Rancho Cañada del Oro Open Space Preserve, 4289 Casa Loma Rd, Morgan Hill, CA

Co-hosted by SJAA and the Santa Clara County Open Space Authority. About 30 minutes south of downtown San Jose, Rancho Cañada del Oro offers significantly darker skies than the city — dark enough to see the Milky Way on good nights. The Moon will be at Last Quarter on May 9 and rises around midnight, giving the whole event window moon-free skies.

⚠️ Registration required — limited to ~95 cars. If you registered and can no longer attend, please cancel so others on the waitlist can take your spot.

  • Arrive no later than 1 hour before the end time (10:15 PM); gates may close after that
  • Sign in at the OSA table when you arrive
  • Binoculars welcome; personal telescopes and tripods not permitted
  • Dress in warm layers — it gets cold at night
  • No pets, no smoking, no flash photography
  • Check the Meetup page before heading out for any weather-related updates
