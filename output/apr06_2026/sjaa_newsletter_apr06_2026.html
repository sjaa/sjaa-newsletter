✨ This Week in the Sky

🌖 Waning Moon — Dark Skies Ahead
The April Full Moon (Pink Moon) rose on April 2nd, and the Moon is now waning toward Last Quarter. By mid-week, skies will be significantly darker in the late evening, building toward the New Moon on April 17 — a great window for deep-sky observing on the horizon.

☿ Mercury in the Morning Sky
Mercury reached its greatest western elongation (27.8° from the Sun) on April 3rd, and this week is still an excellent time to catch it. Look for Mercury low on the eastern horizon just before sunrise. This is one of the best Mercury apparitions of the year — don't miss it!

🌠 Lyrids Meteor Shower — Plan Ahead!
The annual Lyrid meteor shower runs April 16–25, with its peak on the night of April 22/23. The Lyrids typically produce about 20 meteors per hour at peak, with occasional bright dust trails. The nearly first-quarter moon will set shortly after midnight, leaving dark skies for excellent viewing. Produced by debris from Comet C/1861 G1 Thatcher, meteors radiate from the constellation Lyra but can appear anywhere in the sky. Mark your calendars!

🪐 Free Public Talk: First Journey to a Metallic Asteroid

Date: Wednesday, April 8, 2026

Time: 7:00 PM – 8:30 PM PDT

Location: Smithwick Theater, Foothill College, 12345 El Monte Road, Los Altos Hills, CA

Dr. Lindy Elkins-Tanton — Director of UC Berkeley's Space Sciences Laboratory and lead of the NASA Psyche mission — presents "The NASA Psyche mission: First Journey to an Unknown World."

Psyche is the first mission to explore a metallic asteroid — possibly the exposed iron core of an ancient planetesimal. Dr. Elkins-Tanton will discuss what is known about the asteroid, how the mission was designed and built, and the latest mission status. Free and open to the public.

Part of the Silicon Valley Astronomy Lecture Series (now in its 27th year), co-sponsored by Foothill College, the SETI Institute, and the Astronomical Society of the Pacific.
