ðŸŒ  Geminid Meteor Shower Peak - December 13-14

The Geminids are usually one of the best meteor showers of the year! This shower is known for producing bright, colorful meteors and can produce up to 120 meteors per hour at peak under ideal conditions.

  • Peak Night: December 13-14, 2025
  • Best Viewing: After midnight through dawn
  • Moon Phase: Waning gibbous (some interference expected)
  • Radiant: Constellation Gemini

The Geminids are unique because they originate from an asteroid (3200 Phaethon) rather than a comet. Look for meteors radiating from the constellation Gemini, but they can appear anywhere in the sky.

Date: Saturday, December 13, 2025

Time: 6:00 PM - 8:00 PM

Location: Rancho Canada del Oro Open Space Preserve
4289 Casa Loma Rd, Morgan Hill, CA

Join SJAA and Santa Clara County Open Space Authority for an unforgettable night of exploring the night sky! Our knowledgeable docents will guide you through the wonders of our universe at this dark sky site just 30 minutes south of downtown San Jose.

Highlights:

  • View celestial objects through our telescopes
  • Perfect timing to catch the Geminid meteor shower!
  • Dark skies for excellent viewing conditions
  • Family-friendly event (best for ages 5+)
  • Expert SJAA docents to answer your questions

Important Information:

  • Registration Required: Limited to ~85 people due to parking
  • Currently: 102 registered (event may be at capacity)
  • One registration per vehicle (not per person)
  • Arrive no later than 1 hour before end time
  • Sign in at OSA's front table upon arrival
  • Visual observing only (no personal telescopes; binoculars welcome)
  • Event will be cancelled in case of rain, heavy clouds, or thick smoke

What to Bring:

  • Warm layers (it gets cold at night!)
  • Dim flashlight with red filter (or get it wrapped at sign-in)
  • Binoculars (optional)

Please Note:

  • No touching telescopes or eyepieces
  • No flash photography
  • No smoking or pets
