## ✨ THIS WEEK IN THE SKY

🌕 **Full Moon (Flower Moon) — Friday, May 1**
The Moon reaches full phase on May 1 at 17:24 UTC (10:24 AM PDT). Early Native American tribes called this the *Flower Moon*, marking the blooming of spring flowers. Also known as the Corn Planting Moon and the Milk Moon. Great for lunar and planetary viewing, but bright moonlight will wash out fainter deep-sky objects.

🌠 **Eta Aquarids Meteor Shower — Building Toward Peak**
The Eta Aquarids (active April 19 – May 28) are building toward their peak on May 6–7. Produced by debris from Comet Halley, this shower reaches up to 60 meteors/hour at peak; Northern Hemisphere observers can expect ~30/hour. Meteors radiate from Aquarius but can appear anywhere. Best viewed from a dark site after midnight.
