## ✨ THIS WEEK IN THE SKY

🌕🔴 **TOTAL LUNAR ECLIPSE — March 3**
Don't miss the astronomical event of the season! A total lunar eclipse occurs on the night of March 3rd as the Moon passes completely through Earth's dark shadow. Watch the Moon gradually darken and take on a stunning rusty red "blood moon" color — visible with the naked eye or any telescope.

• **Visibility:** North America — watch from your own backyard!
• **Peak:** Moon reaches full eclipse; look for the red color at maximum totality
• ⚠️ This is the **last total lunar eclipse until the very end of 2028** — don't miss it!

🌕 **Full Moon (Worm Moon) — March 3**
The Full Moon reaches peak fullness at 11:39 UTC, coinciding with the eclipse. Early Native American tribes called this the Worm Moon, marking the time when the ground softens and earthworms reappear. Also known as the Crow Moon, Crust Moon, Sap Moon, and Lenten Moon.

☿ **Mercury in the Evening Sky**
Mercury is currently visible low on the western horizon just after sunset. Catch it while you can — Mercury is notoriously brief in its appearances!
